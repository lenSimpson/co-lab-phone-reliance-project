This is why we have brought you a museum that shows the history of phones, while also providing a test that proves our theory. The theory indicates the theme of machine due to the attatchment of technology.

History

Year 2000- First phone with camera/ email function

Sharp J-SH04 becomes the first camera phone on the market but only available in Japan. BlackBerry launch their 857 which support email and web browsing signifying the start of BlackBerry’s reign

Year 2002- First camrea phone Messaging

Europe’s first camera phone, the Nokia 7650, was released. T-Mobile also announced the UK's first picture messaging service available on the Sony Ericsson T68i.

Year 2003- 3G standard

begins to be adopted worldwide, kicking off the age of mobile internet and paving the way for the rise of smartphones. Three released a trio of 3G mobiles: the Motorola A830, the NEC e606 and the NEC e808.

Year 2007- iPhone debut priced at £499

Year 2008- app store and android market created

The invention of both the App Store and the android market allowed companies to develop apps for these devices, which would kick-start the modern-day app culture we know today. (https://www.uswitch.com/mobiles/guides/history-of-mobile-phones/)

Year 2009- Introduction to 4g

networks significantly improved data speeds and paved the way for advanced mobile services, including video streaming and high-speed internet on smartphones. in 2009, o2 demonstrates a 4g connection for the first time. This vastly improved internet speeds when on the move and allowed for seamless streaming from anywhere you could connect to 4G (https://www.uswitch.com/mobiles/guides/history-of-mobile-phones/)

Year 2011/2014- mobile payment

services, such as Apple Pay, Google Wallet, and Samsung Pay, began to transform how people make transactions using their smartphones.

Year 2013- Improvments to security

Fingerprint scanning went mainstream when it launched on the iPhone 5S via the touch button.

Year 2019- Launch of 5g

The first 5G phones and the fifth-generation network both debuted.

Year 2019- Folding phones

Samsung and Huawei introduced foldable smartphones, featuring flexible displays that could be folded or unfolded to provide a larger screen when needed.
